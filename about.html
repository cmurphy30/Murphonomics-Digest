About

The story behind Murphonomics

Welcome to Murphonomics! Whether you came here because I sent you the link or whether you stumbled upon it by accident, I'm glad you're here and I want to share a little about what this project is and why it exists.

It started as a Substack article. I was a new member of the corporate world trying to be a sponge, absorbing as much information as possible. I'd read the Wall Street Journal on the bus ride to work, listen to the Morning Brew at the gym and to Odd Lots on my walk home. I wanted to be plugged in, and with the constant barrage of notable headlines, the news was (and still is) hard to ignore. I found it impossible to focus on any one issue–tariffs, Iran, interest rates, national debt, Israel, AI, unemployment–how is anyone supposed to keep track of and digest information as they're drinking through a firehose? And how is anyone supposed to have an opinion when all the news sources spin conflict in their own ways? In an attempt to organize my thoughts and figure out what I really believe, I wrote The Trump Economy, a short summary of too many economic variables. It admittedly failed at remaining unbiased, but it did help me take a stance on topics that, for too long, I'd only read about in my economics textbooks.

Among the many topics covered in The Trump Economy was AI. While it's undoubtedly one of the most important factors in a modern economy, most experts have thrown their hands up, and I did too. There's no easy way to predict who the winners and losers will be, but as a member of Gen Z, and someone who struggled to find a job after college, it's difficult to ignore the employment shock of college graduates. Following that article, I wanted to learn more. Is the job market slowdown a symptom of fiscal policy, or is AI actually coming for all our jobs? To answer this question I followed the Instagram hype and switched from ChatGPT to Claude and downloaded VS Code.

Murphonomics is the early-stage, explorational answer to all of these questions. It's meant to let me explore the capabilities of AI by trying to solve an issue I've come across myself too much information. I combine information sources into succinct and easy to digest material, help share some educational material, and share an opinion nobody asked for.

I am not without bias, as you will see from my substack, but the information on the dashboard is. Clear and pulled directly from the Federal Reserve, Bureau of Economic Analysis, and Bureau of Labor Statistics. I hope you find the information as easy to understand as building this website was with Claude.
